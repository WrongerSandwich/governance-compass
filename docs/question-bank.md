### Axis 12 — Technology Governance
Precautionary ↔ Innovation-First

**FC-12.1 (Principle)**

| | A → Precautionary (−1.0) | B → Innovation-First (+1.0) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | Don't deploy powerful technology until the risks are well understood | Move forward and correct problems as they emerge |
| Body | Some harms, once done, cannot be undone — caution is the only responsible default. | Excessive caution has costs too — delay itself causes suffering by withholding solutions to known problems. |

**FC-12.2 (Institutional)**

| | A → Precautionary (−1.0) | B → Innovation-First (+1.0) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | Innovators must prove their technology is safe before deployment | Those who want to restrict technology must prove it's dangerous |
| Body | The burden of proof falls on developers, just as it does for pharmaceutical companies. | Innovation should proceed by default — regulation should respond to demonstrated harms. |

**FC-12.3 (Specific)**

| | A → Precautionary (−1.0) | B → Innovation-First (+1.0) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | Don't deploy AI in government if its reasoning can't be explained | Deploy AI in government even if its reasoning isn't fully transparent |
| Body | Decisions affecting people's lives must be made through processes that can be understood and challenged. | The quality of outcomes matters more than whether the process is transparent, especially if the alternative is worse human decision-making. |
